The Sun

"The source of warmth and light"

  • Type - G-type star or yellow dwarf
  • Lifespan - 10 billion years
  • Core fuel - Hydrogen
  • Layer - Corona, Chromosphere, Photosphere, Convection zone, Radiation zone, Core

Mercury

“The closest but not the hottest”

  • The closest planet in the solar system
  • Temperature - 450.C during daytime, -170.C during night time
  • Core - Metallic core

Venus

"Hell like place"

  • Characteristics - Brightest planet in the night sky
  • Atmosphere content - Nitrogen dioxide, sulfur dioxide, carbon dioxide and monoxide.
  • Temperature - 464 degree celcius

Earth

"The perfect place for life"

  • Placement - 1 A.U. or Habitable zone
  • Layer - Crust, Outer mantle, Inner mantle, Outer core, Inner core
  • Atmosphere Layer - Troposphere, Stratosphere, Mesosphere, Thermosphere ( Ionosphere ), Exosphere
  • Moon - The Moon

Mars

“Red planet”

  • Characteristics - Reddest planet in the night sky
  • Atmosphere content - Mostly carbon dioxide
  • Moon - Phobos, Deimos

Asteroid Belt

"A ring of asteroid"

  • A ring of asteroid that splits the terrestrial planet and gas giants

Jupiter

"Earth's hidden shield"

  • Characteristics - Big red spot
  • Components of its atmosphere - Hydrogen and Helium
  • Fun fact - The planet that has the largest ocean and has ring
  • Moon - 95 moons, most known moons ( Europa, Io, Ganymede )

Saturn

“Beautiful ring that you compare it with you girlfriend ”

  • Characteristics - Beautiful Ring
  • Components of its atmosphere - Hydrogen and Helium
  • Fun Fact - Its core is made up from iron and nickel like metal
  • Moon - 146 moons, most known moon ( Titan )

Uranus

"The special one"

  • Axis of self rotation - near 90 degree angle
  • Ring - 13 faint ring
  • Moon - 28 moons, most well known moons ( Miranda, Ariel, Titania, Umbriel, Oberon )

Neptune

“Bluest ice giant”

  • Fun fact - The only planet that could not been seen by naked eye
  • Atmosphere components - Mostly Hydrogen and Helium and a little bit of Methane
  • Moon - 14 moons, most well known moon ( Triton )

Kuiper Belt

“Outer most belt of the solar system”

  • The outermost belt that cover the solar system

Dwarf Planet

“Planet but can't clear trash”

  • Dwarf Planet can't clear trash in its orbit

Oort cloud

“Likely an invisible shield covering the whole solar system”

  • The outermost layer of the solar system
